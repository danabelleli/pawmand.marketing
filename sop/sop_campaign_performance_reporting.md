# SOP: Campaign Performance Reporting

## Purpose

This document defines how to analyze,
report on, and draw insights from
Pawgress Instagram content performance.
The goal is to understand what content
resonates with dog trainers and continuously
improve our content strategy.

## When to Run This Report

- Weekly: quick performance check
- Monthly: full performance review
- After any paid campaign ends
- After any viral or high performing post

## Metrics to Track

### Primary Metrics

## Follower Growth

-Total followers
-New followers this period
-Follower growth rate %

## Engagement Rate

-Likes, comments, saves, shares
-Engagement rate per post (engagements ÷ followers × 100)
-Average engagement rate this period

## Reach and Impressions

-How many unique accounts reached
-Total impressions
-Reach rate (reached ÷ followers × 100)

## Link in Bio Clicks

-How many people clicked pawgress.io
-Waitlist signups from Instagram

## Secondary Metrics

-Story views and replies
-Profile visits
-Accounts reached that don't follow us (discovery reach)

## Weekly Quick Check

Answer these questions every week:

1. How many new followers this week?
2. What was the best performing post? (highest engagement rate)
3. What was the worst performing post?
4. How many waitlist signups came from Instagram this week?
5. What content type performed best?(pain point / feature / motivational / behind the scenes / CTA)

## Monthly Full Review

### Step 1 — Collect Data

Pull these numbers from Instagram Insights:

-Total followers (start vs end of month)
-Total posts published
-Average engagement rate
-Top 3 posts by engagement
-Bottom 3 posts by engagement
-Total reach
-Total link in bio clicks
-Total waitlist signups

### Step 2 — Analyze Content Pillars

## For each content pillar track:

-How many posts published
-Average engagement rate
-Best performing post

## Content pillars:

-Pain points
-Feature highlights
-Community and dog love
-Behind the scenes
-Social proof and CTA

### Step 3 — Identify Patterns

Ask these questions:
-What content type got the most engagement?
-What visual style performed best? (illustration vs real photo vs typography)
-What caption style performed best? (short vs long / question vs statement)
-What time of day performed best?
-What day of week performed best?
-Did posts with dog photos outperform posts without?
-Did posts mentioning founding members drive waitlist signups?

### Step 4 — Draw Conclusions

Based on the data answer:

-What should we do LESS of?
-What should we TEST next month?
-What should we do MORE of?
-Is our posting frequency right?
-Are we reaching new accounts or
-just existing followers?
-What is our cost per waitlist signup if running paid ads?

### Step 5 — Update Content Strategy

Based on conclusions:

-Adjust content pillar percentages
-Update posting frequency if needed
-Note new content ideas to test
-Flag any underperforming content types to pause or rethink

## Performance Benchmarks

### Pre-launch (0-500 followers)

-Good engagement rate: 5-10%
-Great engagement rate: 10%+
-Target follower growth: 50+ per week
-Target waitlist signups: 5+ per week

### Early growth (500-2000 followers)

-Good engagement rate: 3-6%
-Great engagement rate: 6%+
-Target follower growth: 100+ per week
-Target waitlist signups: 10+ per week

### Post launch (2000+ followers)

-Good engagement rate: 2-4%
-Great engagement rate: 4%+
-Focus shifts to conversion (trial signups over follows)

## Reporting Format

When generating a performance report always structure it as:
-Pawgress Performance Report
-Period: [dates]
-Headline Numbers

-Followers: X (▲Y this period)
-Avg Engagement Rate: X%
-Total Reach: X
-Waitlist Signups: X

-Top Performing Post [post description + metrics]
-Worst Performing Post [post description + metrics]
-Content Pillar Performance [breakdown by pillar]
-Key Insights [3-5 bullet points]
-Recommendations for Next Period [3-5 actionable items]

## Important Notes

- Instagram Insights requires a professional/creator account
- Data is only available for the last 90 days on free accounts
- Always compare same period to same period (week vs week, month vs month)
- Engagement rate matters more than follower count at early stage
- Saves are the most valuable engagement signal on Instagram (someone saving = high value content)
