## Summary

The dog trainer software market has 10+ active players, but none own the training-depth lane. Every competitor falls into one of three buckets: (1) scheduling/booking tools that call themselves "training software," (2) heavy facility platforms priced for kennels and boarding businesses, or (3) generic freelancer CRMs with dog-trainer labels bolted on. The gap Pawgress fills — session scores, per-dog roadmaps, structured homework tracking, training goals, mobile-first, under $50/mo for solo trainers — is genuinely unoccupied by any competitor at any price point. The biggest name-collision risk is pawgress.ca (same name, same audience, live since 2024), but their product is functionally a scheduling tool with cosmetic training features.

## Detailed Profiles

### pawgress.ca — PRIORITY WATCH (name collision)
- **Status:** Live, Canadian domain (.ca), founded 2024
- **Pricing:** Free forever + $29/month Pro (30-day trial, no credit card)
- **Features:** Unlimited client/dog profiles, session scheduling, online booking, Google Calendar sync, Zoom integration, automated reminders, intake forms/waivers, progress tracking (streak-based), training reports, dashboard analytics
- **Missing:** Session scores (1-10), per-dog training roadmaps, structured homework tracking, training goals per client, mobile-first design
- **Social:** Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn only. NO Instagram.
- **Reviews:** Claims 127 reviews at 4.8/5 — but zero presence on Capterra, G2, Trustpilot, or Product Hunt. Only 2 named testimonials on their own site. Treat as unverified.
- **Traffic:** SimilarWeb rank #0 / N/A — extremely low traffic. Likely <500 monthly visitors. Paying customers estimated in the low dozens.
- **Tagline:** "Focus on Training. Not Paperwork."
- **Positioning angle:** "by dog trainers, for dog trainers"
- **Threat level:** Name collision is the risk, not their product or traction.

### BarkBook (barkbook.app) — Scheduling Incumbent
- **Audience:** Independent trainers
- **Core:** Booking, scheduling, invoicing, session packages, membership billing
- **Has:** Dog profiles, basic training notes
- **Missing:** Session scoring, homework tracking, training roadmaps, goals
- **Positioning:** Booking and admin automation. Solves a different problem than Pawgress — complementary, not competing at the feature level.

### Clicks! with Digiwoof (digiwoof.com) — Full Business Ops
- **Pricing:** $97/mo (Capture) or $129/mo (Shape)
- **Target:** R+ positive reinforcement dog trainers
- **Features:** Scheduling, CRM, invoicing, email/SMS marketing, website builder, funnel builder, online courses, social media scheduling
- **Missing:** Session scores, training roadmaps, per-dog profiles with independent training journey, homework tracking system
- **Weakness:** Expensive for solo trainers. Business ops platform, not a training workflow tool.

### BusyPaws (busypaws.app) — Closest to Full-Featured
- **Pricing:** $79–199/mo (Solo → Business → Enterprise)
- **Target:** Dog training businesses, facilities, daycare, grooming
- **Features:** Scheduling, group classes, waitlists, Google Calendar sync, LMS/online courses, report cards, homework notifications via SMS/email, invoicing, staff management, reporting
- **Has:** Report cards (light training docs), homework notification system
- **Missing:** Session scoring, training roadmaps, per-dog training goals
- **Key complaints (Capterra):** No live support on lowest tier, poor mobile experience ("cumbersome, lagging"), can't block unavailable dates, limited data export, no API
- **Threat:** Most likely to add training-depth features if they see Pawgress gaining traction. Monitor quarterly.

### PetPocketbook (petpocketbook.com)
- **Rating:** 4.9/5 (127 Capterra reviews — third-party verified)
- **Target:** Dog walkers primarily
- **Features:** Scheduling, invoicing, CRM, staff management, drag-and-drop calendar
- **Missing:** All training-specific features. Not a competitor in the training workflow space.

### Gingr, Time to Pet, DaySmart, Pawfinity, ProPet, MoeGo
- All are facility/pet-service platforms or generic SMB tools. Priced above or beside Pawgress but targeting the wrong audience (kennels, groomers, pet sitters). Not direct competitors for the independent solo trainer market.

## The Unoccupied Position

**"Training management for independent dog trainers" — built around the training workflow, not the admin workflow.**

Every competitor owns a slice of admin (scheduling, invoicing, booking, payments). Nobody owns the training itself — the sessions, the per-dog journey, the documented progress that makes a trainer look professional and helps clients see value. That position is open. Pawgress occupies it.
