# pawgress.ca — Deep Dive: Product, Reviews & Competitive Intel
*Date: 2026-04-13*

---

## Summary

pawgress.ca is a live Canadian dog training management SaaS with the exact same name as Pawgress (.io). They launched in 2024 and offer a free tier plus a $29/month Pro plan. Their product is fundamentally a scheduling and booking tool with cosmetic training features layered on — they do not have session scores, per-dog training roadmaps, or structured homework tracking. Their claimed 127 reviews at 4.8/5 are unverified (no Capterra, G2, Trustpilot, or Product Hunt presence). SimilarWeb shows them at rank #0 with unmeasurably low traffic, suggesting fewer than a few dozen paying customers. The name collision is the real risk — their product and traction are not.

| Per-dog tracking | No roadmap | Full customizable training roadmap per dog |
| Homework | Basic mention in reports | Structured per-session homework tracking |
| Goals | Not present | Top 3 goals per client, tracked |
| Mobile | Not stated | Mobile-first, built for on-the-go trainers |
| Instagram | Zero presence | @getpawgress — own this channel completely |
| Pricing entry | Free tier (feature-limited) | Full features on first paid tier — better value for serious trainers |
| Training focus | "Business management" that includes scheduling | Pure training workflow — sessions, dogs, progress |

### Strategic build priorities (informed by competitor gap)
1. **Make session documentation the product centerpiece** — their reports are generic; yours are professional records
2. **Per-dog roadmaps are your moat** — nothing in the whole market has this
3. **Client-facing progress share (future)** — they have client booking; you'll eventually need a client view
4. **Don't build scheduling** — it's the most crowded feature, not your differentiator
5. **Automated follow-ups are already a differentiator** — make this visible in marketing; they don't prominently feature it

## The Name Situation

They have the .ca domain, Canadian registration, and early indexing on the "pawgress" keyword. A US trademark search should be run immediately. If they haven't filed in the US, you may have a window — but the cost of fighting for a shared name over years (SEO confusion, brand dilution, potential C&D) likely outweighs a clean rename at pre-launch.
