# Outreach Targets: Facebook Groups & Instagram Accounts
**Date: April 9, 2026**
**Purpose:** Pre-launch community building and direct outreach for Pawgress (@getpawgress)

---

## Summary

Professional dog trainer communities are concentrated in a handful of high-trust Facebook groups and a long tail of micro-influencer Instagram accounts (500–10K followers). The Facebook groups below represent the highest-trust channels available — where trainers discuss business tools, frustrations, and recommendations with peers. The Instagram accounts are independent trainers who are active, engaged, and representative of the exact customer Pawgress is built for. Introducing Dana as an honest founder — not a trainer, just a dog lover who built what was missing — is the most credible approach in both channels.

---

## Key Insights

- **Facebook groups are where trust lives.** Trainer communities on Facebook are tight-knit, skeptical of sales pitches, and highly responsive to genuine founders asking real questions. The right introduction in one group can generate more waitlist signups than weeks of Instagram content.
- **Micro-accounts (500–5K) are the priority for outreach.** Larger trainer accounts (50K+) are less likely to respond to a cold DM and less likely to generate the peer-trust conversions that matter at this stage. Accounts in the 1K–8K range are active, reachable, and their audiences trust them deeply.
- **Force-free / R+ / positive reinforcement trainers are the best-fit segment.** They skew more business-savvy, more likely to invest in professional tools, and more likely to engage with a founder story rooted in genuine dog love and ethical craft.
- **"Business & Marketing for R+ Dog Pros" is the single highest-priority Facebook group.** It sits at the exact intersection of training methodology and business operations — the two things Pawgress addresses.

---

## Recommendations

1. **Join "Business & Marketing for R+ Dog Pros" first.** Spend one week reading before posting. This group's members are exactly the Pawgress customer — R+ trainers running their own business and talking openly about the tools they use.
2. **Use the honest founder introduction script** (see pre-launch strategy) in each group after 3–5 days of observation. No links on the first post. Ask about their current admin workflow.
3. **Prioritize Instagram accounts in the 1K–5K follower range** for DM outreach — they're reachable, credible to their audience, and more likely to give genuine feedback.
4. **Engage with each Instagram account for 2 full weeks before sending any DM.** Comment on at least 3–4 posts with genuine reactions. The name recognition makes the DM land as a warm outreach, not a cold pitch.
5. **Ask for feedback, not promotion.** The first DM is always: "I'd love your honest take" — not "here's my product."

## How to Complete the 50-Account List

Instagram's native search + hashtag mining is the most reliable method for finding the right micro-accounts. Use this process:

1. Search `#dogtrainingbusiness` on Instagram
2. Filter to "Recent" posts
3. Click into accounts that look independent and professional
4. Check: follower count (500–8K), posting frequency (3+/week), engagement (comments are real, not just likes)
5. Add to a saved collection on Instagram called "Outreach Queue"
6. Begin engaging with content immediately — like, save, leave a genuine comment
7. DM only after 2 weeks of consistent engagement

Target: 50 accounts in the outreach queue by end of Week 2.
