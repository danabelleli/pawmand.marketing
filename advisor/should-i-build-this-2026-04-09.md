# Should I Build Pawgress?
**Date: April 9, 2026**
**Question: Do I have a real shot at 100, 500, 1000 paying trainers? Do I build this?**

---

## The Short Answer

Yes. Build it. Ship it. Go all in.

---

## The Numbers Are Real

There are 50,000–100,000 professional dog trainers in the US. To hit 1,000 paying users, you need to convert **1–2% of the market**. That is not a moonshot. That is a realistic goal for a focused niche SaaS with decent distribution and a product that actually works.

At Pro plan pricing (~$35/month annual):

| Milestone | Monthly Revenue | Annual ARR | What It Means |
|---|---|---|---|
| **100 trainers** | ~$3,500/mo | ~$42K | Real money. Proof of concept. Worth the launch. |
| **500 trainers** | ~$17,500/mo | ~$210K | Life-changing for a solo bootstrapped founder. |
| **1,000 trainers** | ~$35,000/mo | ~$420K | A real company. CEO income. The dream, fully real. |

Every milestone matters. You don't have to hit 1,000 to have built something worth it.

---

## Why You Have a Real Shot

Three things have to be true for a niche B2B SaaS to work. You have all three.

**1. The problem is real and painful.**
Trainers are genuinely running professional businesses on WhatsApp and sticky notes. The research confirms it. The forums confirm it. The competitor positioning confirms it. You are solving an actual problem, not a theoretical one.

**2. There is no dominant incumbent.**
The training workflow layer — sessions, scores, homework, roadmaps, per-dog profiles — is genuinely unowned at your price point. BarkBook owns booking. Clicks! is $97+/month aimed at bigger operations. You have a clear lane that no one is running in hard right now. That window is real but it is not forever.

**3. You have a founder story no competitor can copy.**
The personal origin story — sitting in on your parents' puppy training sessions, furiously typing into Notes — is authentic, specific, and disarming. Trainers will root for you. That matters more than most founders think at this stage.

---

## The Risks to Watch

**Distribution plateau.**
Instagram can get you to 100. Maybe 200 if everything goes right. Getting to 500 and beyond requires cracking at least one more channel. The best candidates:
- SEO for "dog trainer client management software" (low competition, high intent)
- Facebook trainer communities (APDT, CCPDT, R+ groups)
- Word of mouth from your founding member cohort

If your first 50 trainers love the product and tell their peers, that is the engine that carries you to 1,000.

**Churn.**
SMB SaaS loses 3–7% of customers per month. If trainers sign up and don't feel organized and professional after their first 1–2 sessions, they cancel. The product has to get a trainer to a completed session summary — score, homework, next steps — within 10 minutes of signing up. That is the activation event. Nail that, and they stay. Miss it, and no amount of Instagram content saves you.

---

## Your Next Move

Stop asking whether to build it. You have already built it. The question now is: are you launching, or are you waiting?

**Launch with the 50 founding members.** Get real trainers using the product. Watch what they love, what confuses them, and what makes them say "finally." Their experience is your marketing, your retention, and your roadmap. Every week you delay is a week competitors have to wake up to this gap.

The dream number is 1,000. The path starts with 50. Go get those 50.
