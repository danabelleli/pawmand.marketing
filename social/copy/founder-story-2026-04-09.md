## The Real Story (in Dana's words)

Dana is a software developer who always dreamed of building something of her own. She loves problem solving and creating something from nothing. For a long time she wanted to build a product that brings real value to people's lives — but didn't know what. Every idea that came up wasn't the right one.

She brainstormed topics she genuinely cared about: fitness, dogs, travel. Dogs kept pulling her back — because she has a dog, and her dog is her everything.

She started looking at what she could build in the dog world. Landed on a webapp for dog trainers — because she's organised, she has experience building client management applications, and it was the right combination: something she loves, something she's good at.

She built Pawgress hoping to make a difference — not just for trainers, but for dog owners too. As a dog owner herself, she understands both sides.

**What is NOT true (do not use):**
- Dana did not build Pawgress because of a specific session she sat in on
- She has not yet talked to dog trainers
- The Notes app memory is a real but minor detail from years ago — not the founding moment

### Caption

For a long time I wanted to build something of my own.

Not a side project. Something real — that solves a real problem and makes someone's life a little better.

I just didn't know what.

I sat with it for a long time. Made lists. Brainstormed every topic I cared about — fitness, travel, dogs. Dogs kept coming back. Because my dog is my everything, and the dog world is a world I actually want to be part of.

I looked at what I could build there. And I kept coming back to dog trainers — serious professionals running their own businesses, doing incredible work, with almost no tools built for how they actually work.

I'm a developer. I've built client management tools before. I'm organised. And I love dogs.

It clicked.

So I built Pawgress — a client management platform for dog trainers. Session summaries, per-dog profiles, training roadmaps, homework tracking, progress over time. Built for the training workflow, not repurposed from something generic.

I hope it makes a difference. For trainers, and for the dog owners who trust them with the dogs they love.

Pre-launch now. Waitlist open at pawgress.io. Link in bio. First 50 trainers get 2 months completely free — locked pricing forever. 🐾

## Copywriter Notes

- **The emotional core:** "My dog is my everything" — every dog owner in the audience feels this. It's the bridge between Dana and the trainers she's building for.
- **What makes this story work:** It's the classic "passion meets skill" founding narrative, told honestly. Dana didn't stumble into a problem — she chose a world she loves and found the right problem to solve in it. That's a mark of a thoughtful founder, not a desperate one.
- **What to never add back:** The Notes app session story as a founding moment. It can exist as a supporting memory if relevant in a future post, but it is not why Pawgress exists.
- **Dana as a dog owner:** Her being a dog owner herself is a quiet but powerful detail. She's not just building for trainers — she understands the client side too.
