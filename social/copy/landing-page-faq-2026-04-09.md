## FAQ Copy

---

**Does Pawgress handle invoicing and payments?**

Not yet — and we want to be upfront about that. Right now, Pawgress tracks session costs and gives you a running total per client, so your payment records are always clean and in one place. Full invoicing is on the roadmap and arriving shortly after launch. If you're already using Square, Stripe, or PayPal for payments, Pawgress fits right alongside them — it just handles everything else. The training management piece: fully covered from day one.

---

**Is this built for solo trainers, or for big training facilities?**

Solo trainers, full stop. Most dog training software is built for kennels, boarding facilities, and multi-trainer teams — which is why it never quite fits how you actually work. Pawgress was built from the ground up for independent trainers running their own business. If you're managing your own clients, your own sessions, and your own dogs — this was made for you.

---

**How is Pawgress different from a generic CRM like HoneyBook or Dubsado?**

Generic CRMs don't know what a session score is. They don't know what dog homework looks like. They don't have training roadmaps, per-dog profiles, or a session summary built around how training actually works. You'd be duct-taping a freelancer tool onto a training workflow and making it fit. Pawgress already fits — because it was built specifically for the way you work, not repurposed from something else.

---

**What does the 14-day free trial include?**

Everything. Full access to all features — no restricted mode, no "upgrade to unlock." You get the dashboard, client and dog profiles, session management, roadmaps, homework tracking, progress scores, and Google Calendar sync. No credit card required to start. If Pawgress isn't the right fit, you walk away with no charge and no hassle.

---

**How much does it cost after the trial?**

Three plans, all billed monthly or annually (annual saves you money):

- **Basic** — $25/month (or $20/month billed annually). Up to 10 active clients and 12 dogs.
- **Pro** — $45/month (or $35/month billed annually). Up to 30 clients and 45 dogs. Most popular.
- **Unlimited** — $75/month (or $60/month billed annually). Unlimited clients and dogs.

Every plan includes full access to all features. No locked-out tiers.

---

**What is the Founding Member offer, and how does it work?**

The first 50 trainers to join the waitlist get two months completely free at launch — no trial clock, just free. Plus early access before the public launch, and launch pricing locked in forever. It's our way of saying thank you to the trainers who believed in this before anyone else did. Once those 50 spots are gone, the offer is gone.

---

**I'm not very tech-savvy. Is this going to be hard to set up?**

It's built for trainers who are on the move, not sitting at a desk all day. Setup takes minutes — you add your first client, create a dog profile, and log a session. That's it. No complicated onboarding, no manual to read. If you can use Instagram, you can use Pawgress.

---

**Does it work on my phone?**

Yes — Pawgress is mobile-first. It runs in your browser on any device. No app download required, no waiting for an iOS or Android release. Open it on your phone after a session and fill in the summary while it's still fresh. That's exactly how it was designed to be used.

---

**Does it sync with my calendar?**

Yes. Pawgress syncs with Google Calendar so your scheduled sessions show up where you're already looking.

---

**When is Pawgress launching?**

Soon. The waitlist is open now, and founding members will get early access before the public launch. Follow @getpawgress for updates, or join the waitlist and we'll email you the moment the doors open.

## Copywriter Notes

**Objections addressed and why:**

| Objection | Where It Appears | Why It's Here |
|---|---|---|
| Payment processing gap | Q1 | Strategy flags this as a non-negotiable to address proactively. Silence looks like a gap; transparency looks like a plan. |
| Solo vs. facility fit | Q2 | Market research shows most existing tools are built for facilities. Trainers need to know immediately this is different. |
| Generic CRM comparison | Q3 | HoneyBook, Dubsado actively marketing to dog trainers. Worth naming and neutralizing directly. |
| Trial scope | Q4 | SMB trainers abandon tools fast if onboarding is confusing. Full access + no credit card removes the risk. |
| Pricing | Q5 | Price sensitivity is real. Laying out the tiers clearly prevents sticker shock and positions value. |
| Founding member clarity | Q6 | Scarcity needs to be explained to be believed. "2 months free, locked pricing, early access" needs specifics. |
| Tech complexity | Q7 | SMB churn risk — 43% of churn in first 90 days. Positioning setup as simple and mobile-native lowers the barrier. |
| Mobile access | Q8 | Trainers work on their phone. No app download = no friction point before they've even tried it. |
| Calendar sync | Q9 | Mentioned in product offering. Trainers already live in Google Calendar — this is a trust-builder. |
| Launch timeline | Q10 | Pre-launch waitlist creates urgency gap. Acknowledging "soon" + directing to Instagram keeps them engaged. |

**Decisions to confirm:**
- Payment answer leads with honesty, then pivots to what IS covered. Can rewrite to lead harder on the feature coverage if preferred.
- HoneyBook named directly — this is intentional. If you'd prefer to keep it competitor-agnostic, can make it generic ("generic freelancer tools").
- FAQ order is by friction level — highest objection first.
