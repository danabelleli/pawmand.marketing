## Campaign Brief

### Objective
Introduce the founder behind Pawgress — build emotional connection with professional dog trainers, establish authenticity, and drive waitlist signups. This is a brand awareness + trust-building campaign. Secondary goal: early follower growth through shareability of the founding narrative.

### Audience
Professional dog trainers who run their own business. They're skeptical of software — they've been burned by generic tools that don't understand their world. This campaign speaks to them as peers, not prospects. The emotional hook is "someone built this because they actually love dogs and see the value in what you do."

### Core Message
> *A developer who loves dogs saw how much trainers were doing with how little support — and built something about it.*

The story works because it's honest, specific, and not inflated. Dana didn't have a dramatic founder moment — she had a genuine passion and a real skill match. That's the message.

### Campaign Arc
This is a single-drop mini campaign — not a multi-week rollout. Both pieces go out together (or within 24 hours of each other):

| Order | Format | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Feed carousel | Full story — depth and saves |
| 2 | Story carousel | Highlight-ready summary — reach and retention |

The feed post carries the narrative weight. The story series lives in the "Our Story" Highlight permanently and acts as an always-on brand introduction.

### CTAs
- **Primary:** Join the waitlist → pawgress.io (link in bio)
- **Secondary:** Save this post / Share with a trainer you know

### Budget
Organic only.

### Success Metrics
- Feed carousel saves (target: high save rate — this is a story worth keeping)
- Profile visits from the post
- Link in bio clicks (waitlist signups)
- Story views through to slide 5
- Follows from non-followers who see the post

### Caption

```
For a long time I wanted to build something of my own.

Not a side project. Something real — that solves a real problem and makes someone's life a little better.

I just didn't know what.

I sat with it for a long time. Made lists. Brainstormed every topic I cared about — fitness, travel, dogs. Dogs kept coming back. Because my dog is my everything, and the dog world is a world I actually want to be part of.

I looked at what I could build there. And I kept coming back to dog trainers — serious professionals running their own businesses, doing incredible work, with almost no tools built for how they actually work.

I'm a developer. I've built client management tools before. I'm organised. And I love dogs.

It clicked.

So I built Pawgress — a client management platform for dog trainers. Session summaries, per-dog profiles, training roadmaps, homework tracking, progress over time. Built for the training workflow, not repurposed from something generic.

I hope it makes a difference. For trainers, and for the dog owners who trust them with the dogs they love.

Pre-launch now. Waitlist open at pawgress.io. Link in bio. First 50 trainers get 2 months completely free — locked pricing forever. 🐾

Train. Track. Grow.
```
