# Pawgress Brand Voice Guide

## Voice Overview

Pawgress sounds like a knowledgeable friend who happens to be both a dog lover and a business professional. Not corporate. Not childish. Warm, confident, and real.

## Core Personality Traits

### Warm

We genuinely care about dog trainers and the work they do. Our tone always feels human and approachable — never robotic or transactional.

### Confident

We believe in what we're building. We don't hedge or apologize. We speak with conviction about the problems we solve and the value we provide.

### Relatable

We understand the day to day reality of running a dog training business. We speak their language. We know what
it feels like to juggle clients, dogs, notes, and payments all at once.

### Professional

We're a business tool. Our content reflects that. We're not silly or over the top. We take trainers and
their work seriously.

### Passionate

We love dogs. This isn't just a product — it's built for people who have made dogs their life's work. That passion comes through in everything we say.

## Tone by Content Type

### Pain point posts

-Empathetic and validating.
-"We see you. We get it. There's a better way."
-Never condescending or preachy.

### Feature posts

-Clear and benefit focused.
-Not "we have session notes" but "every session, documented perfectly."
-Show the value not just the feature.

### Motivational posts

-Genuine and grounded.
-Not generic hustle culture.
-Specific to the dog training world.

### CTA posts

-Direct and confident.
-No begging. No desperation.
-"Join the waitlist" not "please follow us we need support"

### Behind the scenes

-Honest and personal.
-Show the real journey of building Pawgress.
-Vulnerability is okay here.

## Writing Rules

### Do:

- Use short punchy sentences
- Start with a strong hook
- Speak directly to the trainer
  ("you", "your clients", "your dogs")
- Use 🐾 emoji sparingly but naturally
- Reference real trainer pain points
- Keep captions scannable
- End with a clear action or thought

### Never:

- Use corporate jargon
- Say "utilize" — say "use"
- Say "leverage" — say "use"
- Say "solution" — say what it actually does
- Use exclamation marks excessively
- Sound desperate or needy
- Be generic — always tie back to dog training specifically
- Use hashtag spam — maximum 10 relevant hashtags per post

## Caption Structure

Hook → Value/Story → CTA

Hook: First line must stop the scroll
Value: Deliver on the hook's promise
CTA: One clear next step
